Beginning

The German Tiger I and the Soviet T-34 are generally considered to be the best tanks of the Second World War. However, at the end of the war, new constructions appeared, with more and more powerful firepower and stronger armor.

Comet (A34) Cruiser Tank

The characteristics of the vehicle
Feature Content
Weight 32,7t
Length 7,65m
Height 2,67m
Speed max 52km/h

The British Comet tank was first used at the end of World War II when it participated in combat in western Germany. It was a development of the Cromwell tank design. The vehicle was equipped with an excellent cannon that was effective against the German Panthers and Tiger. Its construction was so successful that it was used by the British army long after the end of World War II

M26 Pershing

The characteristics of the vehicle
Feature Content
Weight 41,76t
Length 8,32m
Height 2,74m
Speed max 32km/h

The M26 Pershing was named after Army General John J. Pershing, who commanded the American forces in Europe during the First World War. It was used during the fighting in Germany then during Korean War. This clearly shows that when it was put into combat, it was extremely modern construction.

Pershing was transported to Europe in January 1945. They took part in the fighting in the Ardennes. They were also used to capture the Rhine bridge at Remagen and in the storming of Cologne.
